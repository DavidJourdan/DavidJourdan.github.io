Skeletal Skinning with Optimized Centers of Rotation

Final year research project

Skeletal skinning is a technique that is massively used in computer graphics to animate meshes in real-time. The main methods (LBS & DQS) are known to produce artifacts, but in a SIGGRAPH 2016 paper, Le et al. proposed a method almost free of any artifacts. We implemented these 3 methods in a single, multi-view application that can be used to compare them. You can access it here. We also propose (modest) improvements on the original paper.
